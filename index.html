About

I enjoy building systems where hardware, software, and physical behavior intersect. My recent work spans autonomous robotic platforms and a self-directed study of analog integrated circuit design using Cadence Virtuoso and Spectre, with an emphasis on developing intuition rather than following cookbook designs.

Featured Projects

Autonomous Surface Inspection & Coating Rover

Capstone project · ZeroG Coating

  • Designing a mobile robotic system for autonomous surface inspection and coating.
  • Using LiDAR and wheel encoders to execute repeatable coverage paths with closed-loop control.
  • Exploring self-supervised learning to pretrain a vision backbone before task-specific fine-tuning.

TrashAuto — Autonomous Garbage Collection Rover

  • Built a multi-sensor rover integrating LiDAR, ultrasonic sensing, wheel encoders, and vision.
  • Implemented staged perception and decision logic to reduce unnecessary inference.
  • Focused on system robustness and repeatability rather than raw model accuracy.

Cadence Virtuoso Self-Learning — Analog IC Design Foundations

Independent study focused on tool fluency and analog intuition.

  • Learning end-to-end Cadence Virtuoso and Spectre workflows: schematic capture, simulation setup, and result interpretation.
  • Studying and simulating fundamental analog building blocks including bias networks, current mirrors, differential pairs, and single-stage amplifiers.
  • Using DC, AC, and transient analyses to understand gain, bandwidth, stability, and non-ideal device behavior.
