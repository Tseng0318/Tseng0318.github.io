About

Featured Projects

Autonomous Surface Inspection & Coating Rover (Capstone)

AI-driven edge-deployed corrosion detection system · ZeroG Coating

  • Fine-tuning a pretrained ResNet-18 backbone for surface condition classification under real-world lighting and texture variability.
  • Replacing standard softmax decision rules with centroid-distance–based embedding comparison to enable unknown-class rejection.
  • Applying model compression techniques (pruning and quantization) to reduce memory demand and promote efficiency for edge device.
  • Integrating LiDAR, IMU, wheel encoders to execute repeatable coverage paths with closed-loop control.

  • Implemented clock division and debouncing logic to ensure stable user input handling.

  • Verified functionality through simulation.
  • Considered timing behavior and state transitions to ensure reliable hardware execution.

TrashAuto — Autonomous Garbage Collection Rover

Multi-sensor autonomous robotic platform integrating perception, control, and mechanical design.

  • Designed and implemented a two staged CNN pipeline to reduce false prediction in real world application.
  • Used LiDAR for object detection, followed by vision-based classification.
  • Integrated ultrasonic sensing for height validation to prevent misclassification of tall non-garbage objects.
  • Implemented closed-loop navigation using wheel encoders and IMU feedback for precise approach and stopping control.
  • Designed the complete rover chassis and pickup mechanism in Fusion 360, accounting for sensor placement, stability, and actuation constraints.

Cadence Virtuoso Self-Learning — Analog IC Design with TSMC PDK

Independent study focused on tool fluency and analog intuition.

  • Learning end-to-end Cadence Virtuoso and Spectre workflows: schematic, simulation setup, and result interpretation.
  • Studying and simulating fundamental analog building blocks including bias networks, current mirrors, differential pairs, amplifiers, and oscillator.
  • Using DC, AC, and transient analyses to understand gain, bandwidth, stability, and non-ideal device behavior.
  • Verifying circuit behavior using a TSMC CMOS process design kit to evaluate realistic device characteristics rather than idealized models.
  • Analyzing device-level parameters such as threshold voltage, and parasitic capacitances to understand real-world performance limits.
